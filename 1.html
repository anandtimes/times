Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about studying in Malaysia.

How much does it cost to study in Malaysia? +
Tuition fees in Malaysia range from RM 3,000 to RM 90,000 per year depending on the university and program. Living expenses typically cost between RM 1,000 to RM 2,500 per month, making Malaysia one of the most affordable study destinations in Asia.
Can I work part-time while studying? +
Yes, international students are allowed to work part-time up to 20 hours per week during semester breaks and holidays. You need approval from your institution and the Immigration Department.
What is the student visa process? +
After receiving your offer letter from the university, they will apply for your student pass. The process typically takes 4-8 weeks. You'll need to provide medical reports, passport copies, and other required documents.
Is IELTS mandatory for admission? +
Most universities require IELTS (minimum 5.5-6.0) or TOEFL scores. However, some universities offer conditional admission or English language courses if you don't meet the requirement initially.
What is the intake period for Malaysian universities? +
Malaysian universities typically have 2-3 intakes per year: January/February, May/June, and September/October. Some universities offer flexible intake options throughout the year.
Are Malaysian degrees recognized internationally? +
Yes, Malaysian degrees are recognized globally. Many universities have international accreditations, and branch campuses offer degrees from UK and Australian universities.
What are the living conditions like for international students? +
Malaysia offers excellent living conditions with modern infrastructure, affordable accommodation, diverse food options, efficient public transport, and a safe environment. Most universities provide on-campus or nearby housing options.
Do I need a scholarship to study in Malaysia? +
While not mandatory, many universities offer scholarships based on academic merit. Malaysian education is already affordable compared to Western countries, making it accessible even without scholarships.
